# Getting Started with SyGMA

This guide will walk you through using the SyGMA workflow to generate and analyze potential metabolites of chemicals.

## Step 1: Access the SyGMA Workflow

1. Navigate to the **Workflows** section in your ToxIndex dashboard
2. Click on **SyGMA** to open the metabolite generation interface
3. You'll see the SyGMA setup page with options for chemical input and pathway selection

## Step 2: Input Chemical Structures

### Option A: Upload Chemical Files
1. Click **Upload Chemical Structures**
2. Upload SDF, MOL, or SMILES files containing your chemicals
3. Verify the structures are correctly parsed and displayed

### Option B: Draw Chemical Structures
1. Click **Draw Chemical Structure**
2. Use the molecular editor to draw your chemical structure
3. Save the structure for analysis

### Option C: Enter SMILES Strings
1. Click **Enter SMILES**
2. Input SMILES strings for your chemicals
3. Validate the structures before proceeding

## Step 3: Configure Metabolic Pathways

1. **Select Metabolic Pathways**:
   - Phase I metabolism (oxidation, reduction, hydrolysis)
   - Phase II metabolism (conjugation reactions)
   - Environmental transformation pathways
   - Custom metabolic rules

2. **Set Generation Parameters**:
   - Maximum number of transformation steps
   - Metabolite generation depth
   - Filtering criteria for metabolites
   - Similarity thresholds for metabolite clustering

3. **Choose Analysis Options**:
   - Property prediction for metabolites
   - Activity assessment
   - Toxicity prediction
   - Bioavailability estimation

## Step 4: Run Metabolite Generation

1. Click **Start Metabolite Generation**
2. Monitor the progress in the task queue
3. The system will systematically generate potential metabolites

## Step 5: Review Generated Metabolites

### Metabolite Results
- **Generated Metabolites**: List of all potential metabolites
- **Metabolic Pathways**: Step-by-step transformation pathways
- **Metabolite Properties**: Predicted properties and activities
- **Pathway Visualization**: Interactive metabolic pathway diagrams

### Analysis Options
- **Metabolite Prioritization**: Rank metabolites by importance
- **Property Analysis**: Analyze metabolite properties
- **Activity Prediction**: Predict biological activities
- **Toxicity Assessment**: Assess potential toxicity

## Step 6: Explore Metabolic Pathways

### Pathway Visualization
- **Interactive Diagrams**: Click to explore metabolic pathways
- **Step-by-Step Transformations**: Detailed transformation mechanisms
- **Enzyme Information**: Associated enzymes and cofactors
- **Reaction Conditions**: Optimal conditions for transformations

### Pathway Analysis
- **Metabolic Flux**: Analysis of metabolic flow
- **Rate-Limiting Steps**: Identification of bottlenecks
- **Alternative Pathways**: Exploration of alternative routes
- **Pathway Comparison**: Compare different metabolic routes

## Step 7: Export and Save Results

1. **Download Metabolites**: Export SDF files with metabolite structures
2. **Save Pathways**: Export pathway diagrams and data
3. **Generate Reports**: Create comprehensive analysis reports
4. **Share Results**: Share findings with collaborators

## Tips for Success

- **Start with Simple Structures**: Begin with well-characterized chemicals
- **Choose Relevant Pathways**: Select pathways appropriate for your system
- **Review Generated Metabolites**: Validate chemically reasonable metabolites
- **Use Filtering Options**: Apply filters to focus on relevant metabolites

## Troubleshooting

If you encounter issues:

1. **Check Chemical Structures**: Ensure structures are valid and complete
2. **Verify Pathway Selection**: Make sure you've selected at least one pathway
3. **Review Error Messages**: Check the task details for specific error information
4. **Contact Support**: Reach out if you need assistance with the SyGMA workflow
